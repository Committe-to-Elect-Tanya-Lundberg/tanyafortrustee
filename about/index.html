About Me

Parent. Lawyer. Leader.

My mom raised my younger sister and me in Ferndale, a diverse and welcoming community in Oakland County. We didn’t have a lot, but my mom impressed upon us the importance of education, hard work, and dreaming big. I decided at a young age that I would be an attorney when I grew up. I earned a bachelor of arts degree in political science from the University of Michigan-Dearborn, where my husband Jeff and I met. At UM-D, I worked on the school newspaper, serving as the editor-in-chief for three semesters and earning a Distinguished Student Leader award. I then earned my juris doctor, cum laude, at Wayne State University Law School. At Wayne State, I served as an assistant editor on Law Review and was elected secretary of the Student Board of Governors (SBG), the law school’s student council. I earned the best assistant editor award on Law Review and the president’s award for outstanding contributions to SBG.

I spent almost nine years in private practice, first in commercial litigation, then in insurance defense. I joined the Women’s Bar Association (WBA), the Oakland Region of the Women Lawyers Association of Michigan (WLAM), and became active in the Oakland County Bar Association. I earned a WLAM Regional Leadership Award and served as WBA President (2018-2019). I currently serve as a Director at Large for WLAM.

In 2016, I transitioned to the Career Services Office at University of Detroit Mercy School of Law, where I am now the Assistant Dean. My team and I help Detroit Mercy Law students and alumni with their job search strategy, applications, practice interviews, and professional development. In addition to WLAM, I am active in the Federal Bar Association – Eastern District of Michigan Chapter and serve as the Vice Chair (Membership) of the Solo & Small CSO Section for the National Association for Law Placement. In 2017, I earned the Detroit Mercy Commitment to Excellence “Rising Star” award, based on my contributions to the Career Services Office and the School of Law. I am also a three-time finalist for Administrator of the Year, voted on by our law students.

In my free time, I follow college football, men’s and women’s basketball, and softball (Go Blue!), read contemporary fiction (love my Kindle app), cook and bake, and walk for fitness. We enjoy playing card games (I am always looking for euchre tournaments) and I recently discovered the joys of planting annuals and kayaking. Jeff and I have been married for 14 years and he is my best friend and biggest supporter.

I never back down from a challenge. I make decisions and work hard to see them through. I look for creative solutions to problems and accept and incorporate feedback from stakeholders. People who know me well describe me as reliable, determined, honest, and strong. I ran as a write-in candidate in the August primary because I am confident my skills and strengths will make me an asset to our Township government. Now that I’m on the November ballot, I am asking you to vote for me and give me a chance to make a difference for our community.

Why Northville Township

Jeff and I chose Northville Township based on its amenities, schools, and location, and we would choose it again in a heartbeat. We love putting our chairs out for the 4th of July parade, eating and shopping in downtown Northville, and supporting our son’s Stallions football team. Our son has attended Northville Public Schools since preschool and we have both volunteered in his classrooms and contributed to the PTA. We are close to our jobs and Ann Arbor, where we enjoy attending University of Michigan athletics events.

Northville Township is filled with great people and great opportunities.
