Hello from Tanya

Hi there! I’m Tanya Lundberg and I am running for Northville Township Trustee.

My husband, Jeff, and I moved to Northville Township in 2009, just before our son was born. We wanted to find a place where we could plant roots, raise our son, and someday retire. When we walked into our house with our realtor, it was like a movie – we knew we had found our family’s home. We’ve enjoyed living here ever since and are invested in ensuring that Northville is a thriving place for all residents and visitors.

As an attorney and leader in the metro Detroit legal community, I’ve developed important skills that will make me an effective Township Trustee, including analytical, negotiation, problem-solving, research, and relationship-building skills. I’m running for the opportunity to put these skills to work for our community and be the voice that’s been missing from our Township Board.

Thank you for visiting my website and I hope I earn your vote.

Tanya

Issues

Tanya and her colleagues helping to “keep Belle Isle beautiful”.

Environment

We must protect our community from environmental threats, such as Arbor Hills landfill. The landfill impacts our health, our property values, and our overall quality of life. I will work with my Board colleagues and the Conservancy Initiative, Ridge Wood parents, and government officials at the county, state, and federal level to do everything we can to keep Arbor Hills in check and minimize its impact on our community.

7-Mile Proposal

Development

Northville Twp. is attractive to residential and commercial developers, but we are running out of space. I will work with my Board colleagues to ensure that we are thoughtful about continued residential development and that we bring in commercial development that will enhance our community and quality of life, as well as ensuring we include green space in our plans.

Inclusion

Northville Twp. attracts diverse residents and visitors. We must be intentional about making sure all of our residents and visitors feel welcomed and valued in our community. I will work to cultivate deliberate inclusion at all levels of Township government, support unconscious bias training for all Township employees, and implement community-based initiatives to support inclusion in our neighborhoods.

Anyone can vote safely and quickly before the Nov. 3 general election.

Remember, Michiganders voted in no-reason absentee voting in 2018! Anyone can vote safely and quickly before the November 3 general election. Contact the Clerk’s Office to request an absentee ballot. Absentee ballots will be mailed after September 19 and must be received by the Clerk’s Office by 8pm on November 3. Please drop your absentee ballot off at Township Hall — there is a 24-hour dropbox outside. If you mail your ballot to the Clerk’s Office, you can track it on the Secretary of State’s website to make sure it’s received on time.
